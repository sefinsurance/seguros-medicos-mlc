## Medicare Updates for 2026: What You Should Know

If you or someone you care about is on Medicare, a few changes are on the horizon for 2026. A quick review can go a long way—let’s walk through what’s coming and what it may mean for you.

For 2026, Medicare will adjust some of its costs. For folks using hospital coverage (Part A), deductibles and, for some, monthly premiums will be going up. The same goes for Part B, which covers outpatient care. These aren’t major changes for most, but they’re worth noting if you budget carefully for your healthcare each year.

A big improvement is coming to prescription drug plans (Part D). Once you reach a set out-of-pocket amount—capped at $2,100—your plan will pick up the rest of your approved medication costs for the year. This means predictable medication costs if you take several prescriptions.

Six states (Arizona, New Jersey, Ohio, Oklahoma, Texas, and Washington) will be starting a pilot program where Medicare may require pre-approval for some medical services under Part B. The goal is to make sure services are truly needed and to help reduce unnecessary procedures.

With these updates, your out-of-pocket expenses could look a bit different, and if you live in one of those pilot states, you may need to get some treatments approved ahead of time. On the bright side, folks with higher medication costs should see some relief thanks to the new Part D cap.

Plans can shift things like networks and covered medications year to year, and coverage varies by area and circumstance. Touching base with your Medicare choices each year is a smart way to make sure you’re still getting what you need—and to avoid future surprises.
